# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Finance staff, administrators, and ordinary employees use this private invoice workspace during recurring operational work: uploading invoice originals, monitoring OCR recognition, correcting extracted fields, finding duplicates, and exporting structured invoice data. Ordinary users mainly manage their own invoices; finance and admin users need cross-user visibility, provider settings, quota alerts, and export workflows.

## Product Purpose

The product centralizes invoice files and OCR results so teams can move from scattered originals to searchable, corrected, exportable invoice records. Success means invoices are uploaded safely, recognized asynchronously, reviewed with preserved OCR provenance, checked for duplicates, and exported without exposing provider credentials or sensitive secrets.

## Brand Personality

Calm, exacting, operational. The interface should feel trustworthy and work-focused, with enough polish to reduce cognitive load but no decorative drama that distracts from financial review tasks.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-page composition, oversized hero sections, decorative card grids, glassy dashboards, generic SaaS gradients, or finance cliches built around navy/gold prestige. Avoid any UI that displays Tencent SecretId/SecretKey after entry, logs full OCR payloads casually, or makes quota warnings feel like optional decoration.

## Design Principles

- Make the next operational action obvious: review, retry, confirm, archive, export, or configure.
- Preserve provenance: keep OCR values, manual corrections, provider request IDs, and export metadata visible where decisions depend on them.
- Prefer dense but calm information architecture over illustrative layout.
- Treat warnings as workflow signals, especially OCR quota, failed recognition, duplicate suspicion, and credential status.
- Use familiar controls and restrained visual hierarchy so finance users can scan, compare, and repeat actions quickly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast for text and controls. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, readable Chinese labels, reduced-motion preferences, and color-independent state communication for warning, error, success, and active states.
